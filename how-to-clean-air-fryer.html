How to Clean an Air Fryer: Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

A dirty air fryer doesn't just look bad — it affects food taste, causes smoking, and can even be a fire hazard. Here's exactly how to clean every part of your air fryer properly.

How Often to Clean

PartFrequency
Basket & crisper plateAfter every use
Interior cavityWeekly
Heating elementMonthly
ExteriorWeekly
Deep cleanMonthly

What You'll Need

  • Dish soap (Dawn works best for grease)
  • Baking soda
  • Soft sponge or cloth
  • Old toothbrush or soft brush
  • Paper towels
  • White vinegar (optional)

Step 1: Clean the Basket (After Every Use)

  1. Unplug the air fryer and let it cool for 10 minutes
  2. Remove the basket and crisper plate
  3. Soak in hot soapy water for 10-15 minutes
  4. Scrub with a soft sponge — avoid metal scrubbers
  5. Rinse and dry completely before reinserting

For stubborn grease: Make a paste of baking soda + water. Apply to the greasy area, let sit for 10 minutes, then scrub gently.

Dishwasher safe? Most baskets are top-rack dishwasher safe, but hand washing extends the life of the non-stick coating.

Step 2: Clean the Interior (Weekly)

  1. Unplug and let cool completely
  2. Flip the air fryer upside down (carefully!)
  3. Wipe the interior with a damp cloth and mild detergent
  4. Pay special attention to the area around the heating element
  5. Wipe dry with a clean cloth

Warning: Never submerge the air fryer body in water. The electronics will be damaged.

Step 3: Clean the Heating Element (Monthly)

The heating element collects grease over time, which causes smoking and reduces heating efficiency.

  1. Unplug and flip upside down
  2. Use a soft brush or old toothbrush to gently scrub the element
  3. Wipe with a damp cloth and mild detergent
  4. Let dry completely before using

If the element is very greasy: Make a paste of baking soda + water. Apply with a soft cloth, let sit 5 minutes, then wipe clean.

Step 4: Remove Burnt-On Grease

For tough, burnt-on grease that won't come off with regular cleaning:

  1. Make a paste: 2 tablespoons baking soda + 1 tablespoon water
  2. Apply the paste to the burnt areas
  3. Let sit for 15-20 minutes
  4. Scrub gently with a soft sponge
  5. Rinse and dry

For extreme cases: Spray with white vinegar, let sit 5 minutes, then apply the baking soda paste. The fizzing reaction helps loosen stubborn grease.

Step 5: Clean the Exterior (Weekly)

  1. Wipe down with a damp cloth
  2. For stainless steel: use a microfiber cloth and stainless steel cleaner
  3. Clean the digital display with a dry microfiber cloth
  4. Don't spray cleaner directly on the unit — spray the cloth first

Step 6: Remove Bad Smells

If your air fryer smells like old food:

  1. Baking soda method: Place an open box of baking soda inside the unplugged air fryer overnight. Remove and wipe clean in the morning.
  2. Lemon method: Cut a lemon in half, place both halves in the basket, and run at 350°F for 10 minutes. The citrus neutralizes odors.
  3. Vinegar method: Wipe the interior with a cloth dampened with white vinegar. Let dry completely.

Common Cleaning Mistakes

  1. Using metal utensils — Scratches the non-stick coating. Use silicone or wooden utensils only.
  2. Not drying completely — Water spots can cause rust on some models.
  3. Cleaning while hot — Can cause thermal shock and damage the coating.
  4. Using abrasive cleaners — Scratches the surface. Use baking soda paste instead.
  5. Ignoring the heating element — Grease buildup causes smoking and fire hazards.
  6. Submerging the body — Water damages the electronics.

How to Prevent Grease Buildup

  1. Use a lower temperature — Higher temps = more grease splatter
  2. Don't overfill — Grease splatters when food is too close to the element
  3. Use parchment paper liners — Catches grease and makes cleanup easier
  4. Wipe after every use — 30 seconds of wiping prevents hours of scrubbing later
  5. Use an oil spray bottle — More controlled than pouring oil

Final Tips

  • Clean after every use — it takes 2 minutes and prevents hours of deep cleaning later
  • Never use metal utensils or scrubbers
  • Always let the air fryer cool before cleaning
  • Dry completely before storing
  • Replace the basket if the non-stick coating is peeling (food safety concern)

A clean air fryer lasts longer, cooks better, and doesn't smoke. Take care of it and it'll take care of your meals.
