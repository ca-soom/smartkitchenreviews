Air Fryer Not Heating? Here's How to Fix It (2026)

Your air fryer stopped heating up. Before you throw it away and buy a new one, try these fixes — most heating issues can be resolved at home in under 10 minutes.

Fix 1: Check the Power Connection (1 minute)

This sounds obvious, but it's the most common cause:

  1. Make sure the plug is fully inserted into the outlet
  2. Try a different outlet (the original might have a tripped breaker)
  3. Check if the outlet works with another appliance
  4. Inspect the power cord for damage or kinks

If the outlet doesn't work: Check your circuit breaker panel. Air fryers draw 1,200-1,800 watts and can trip a breaker if shared with other high-draw appliances.

Fix 2: Check the Basket Placement (1 minute)

Most air fryers have a safety switch that prevents heating if the basket isn't fully inserted:

  1. Remove the basket completely
  2. Reinsert it firmly until you hear a click
  3. Make sure it's seated flat and level
  4. Try running the air fryer again

Pro tip: Clean the basket rails — food debris can prevent the basket from seating properly.

Fix 3: Clean the Heating Element (10 minutes)

A dirty heating element is the #1 cause of reduced heating performance:

  1. Unplug the air fryer and let it cool completely
  2. Flip it upside down (carefully)
  3. Use a soft brush or old toothbrush to gently scrub the heating element
  4. Wipe with a damp cloth and mild detergent
  5. Let it dry completely before using

Warning: Never use metal utensils or abrasive cleaners on the heating element.

Fix 4: Check for Grease Buildup (5 minutes)

Excess grease can cause smoking and prevent proper heating:

  1. Remove the basket and crisper plate
  2. Check the bottom of the air fryer cavity for grease pools
  3. Wipe clean with a paper towel
  4. Wash the basket and crisper plate with hot soapy water
  5. Run the air fryer empty at 400°F for 10 minutes to burn off residue

Fix 5: Reset the Thermal Fuse (Medium difficulty)

Some air fryers have a resettable thermal fuse:

  1. Unplug the air fryer
  2. Look for a small red button on the back or bottom (check your manual)
  3. Press and hold for 5 seconds
  4. Plug back in and test

If there's no reset button: The thermal fuse may be blown and needs replacement. This requires opening the unit — only do this if you're comfortable with basic electronics.

Fix 6: Check the Temperature Sensor (Medium difficulty)

The temperature sensor (thermistor) tells the air fryer when to heat:

  1. Unplug and let cool
  2. Locate the sensor (usually a small probe inside the cavity)
  3. Make sure it's not bent, damaged, or covered in food residue
  4. Gently clean with a damp cloth
  5. If damaged, it needs replacement (contact manufacturer)

Fix 7: When to Replace

If none of the above fixes work, the heating element itself may be burned out. At this point:

  • If under warranty: Contact the manufacturer for a free replacement
  • If 1-2 years old: Consider repairing (heating element replacement costs $20-40)
  • If 3+ years old: Buy a new air fryer — repair costs approach replacement costs

How to Prevent Heating Issues

  1. Clean after every use — Grease buildup is the #1 cause of heating problems
  2. Don't overcrowd the basket — Forces the heating element to work harder
  3. Let it cool before cleaning — Thermal shock can damage the element
  4. Use the right outlet — Don't share with other high-draw appliances
  5. Don't run it empty for too long — 5-10 minutes max for preheating
